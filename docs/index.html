The Brain / Body Seam

architecture · v4

Reflexes are free. Idle fidgets, timers, auto-stop, reconnects — local algorithms, $0, no API call. Cognition (interpreting speech) is the only thing that earns a Gemini round-trip. Audio is an interrupt to an otherwise algorithmic idle loop.

The Phase 3 test is already written. Everything droid-specific lives in one body object; the system prompt is generated from body.actions(). Swapping BB-8 for custom hardware means one new object — if it takes more, that's an architecture violation by definition.

CO-600 · The Flagship

design brief · engineering

A pet that isn't biological, gives the emotional weight of one, has real utility — and can genuinely die.

The flagship form is a buoyancy-assisted airship: a helium envelope carries the static weight, two tilting rotors only steer and trim. It's the one configuration that is quiet, endures for hours, is soft enough to bump into, and fails gently — a power loss becomes a slow sink, not a fall.

Lift Budget 60 CM SPHERE

Lift is a volume game: ~1.0 g of lift per liter of air displaced. A 60 cm envelope — the width of a 27″ monitor — is near the smallest that flies a real payload.

Displaced volume113 L
Gross lift≈ 113 g
Envelope + gondola64 g
Spare lift → battery/ballast≈ 49 g
Target buoyancy fractionf ≈ 0.97

Control Architecture TWO LOOPS

Spinal cord — onboard, 50–100 Hz. Attitude, altitude, and optical-flow drift-kill. Never depends on the phone.

Cortex — on the phone, 5–15 Hz. Vision, personality, cognition. Returns setpoints only — bearing, range, height. The ship never needs to know what a person is; it chases setpoints.

The slow dynamics of a blimp are exactly what make phone-offloaded cognition possible.

Build Tiers EACH DEMOS ALONE

  • Hover + hold — onboard only, no phone.
  • Follow the beacon — UWB/BLE pursuit. Still no vision.
  • See you — camera → phone → detect → orient. The "it looks at you" moment.
  • Understand you — perception feeds the personality brain.

Visual Direction OSPREY, NOT GUNSHIP

Soft body, hard hardware. A quilted charcoal envelope that catches light like a bomber-jacket panel; thick tapered carbon booms into machined collars; a dense gimballed sensor head slung below with a soft amber status ring.

Reconnaissance aircraft meets Teenage Engineering. A creature in gear — not a machine of war.

Grounded in three research platforms that bracket the design: ETH Zurich's nano-blimp (hovering on <200 mW), Georgia Tech's GT-MAB (>2 h endurance), and TU Delft's BEAVIS (55 cm, reliable indoors).
